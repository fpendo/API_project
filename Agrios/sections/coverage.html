Blanket the farm with a solar LoRa mesh

The Milesight SG50-class gateways spec 2 km (built-up) to 15 km (rural) range, so the recommended 750 m design is deliberately conservative — every point sits inside two or more cells for redundancy. A strict 200 m assumption (worst-case dense canopy) leaves gaps and would need extra in-fill nodes; the mesh + raising antennas on short masts closes that easily on open moorland.

Why the circles overlap on purpose. Overlap isn't waste — it's resilience. Every gateway can hear the herd in its neighbour's cell, so if one node is shaded by a combe, blocked by weather, or down for maintenance, traffic simply re-routes through the mesh to the hub. No single point of failure across the grazing platform.
How it works & what it costs

The network, the herd, and the bill of materials

Indicative UK pricing for June 2026, ex-VAT, based on current market hardware. Starting with a 50-head herd on ~500 acres.

1 · The LoRa gateways

Five solar, off-grid LoRaWAN gateways — four corners + centre.

Each gateway is an 8-channel solar LoRaWAN gateway (Milesight SG50-class): built-in solar panel + battery (≈4 days autonomy without sun), IP67, with 2–15 km rural range. They listen for every EID/sensor uplink in their cell and forward it on. Because they're solar they need no trenching for power — they just need a clear-ish spot and a short mast.

2 · The smart-mesh retrofit

Turning isolated gateways into one self-healing network.

Out of the box each gateway would need its own cellular SIM to reach the internet. Instead we retrofit a smart mesh: a small 5 GHz mesh/point-to-point radio (Ubiquiti airMAX-class) on each gateway so they relay IP traffic peer-to-peer back to the central hub. The mesh is self-healing — if one hop drops, traffic reroutes automatically. Only the hub needs Starlink; no SIM per node. An optional Meshtastic layer adds ultra-low-power, infrastructure-free text alerts (gate, mortality, escape) as a backup channel.

3 · The herd — 50 EID devices

Identity for every animal, live location across the farm.

There are two layers. Mandatory identity EID tags (BCMS-compliant, ~£2–£4 each) give each animal a unique ID and are read at the weigh-crate and race. For live location across 500 acres, LoRa solar GPS ear tags (≈£59 each) report position, movement and behaviour straight to the gateways — no per-tag SIM needed. Starting with a 50-head herd keeps the CapEx manageable while proving the full system.

4 · The central hub & Starlink

The brain of the farm, wired to the sky.

At the farmhouse, a small edge server runs the LoRaWAN network server (self-hosted ChirpStack), the vault sync, the Telegram bot and Whisper transcription. All gateway traffic arrives here over the mesh and goes out to the cloud/LLM via Starlink — the only internet uplink the farm needs. A UPS and small solar keep it alive through outages.
