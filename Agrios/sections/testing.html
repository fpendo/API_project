Testing costs and annual budget

Indicative UK 2026 ex-VAT planning assumptions for a 50-head, 500-acre operation. Genetics is a one-off investment per animal; all other rows recur annually.

ProgrammeAssumptionCost
Soil chemistry + carbon (1 quadrant/yr, 4-yr rotation)~10 composites/yr~£700/yr
Forage — silage lots + feeding season spot checks~7 samples/yr~£280/yr
Water — 4 troughs biannual + borehole annual~10 samples/yr~£350/yr
Animal health — FEC, blood profiles, disease screen~50 tests/yr~£900/yr
Recurring annual testing budget~£2,230/yr
Genetics — WGS (5 animals) + 50K SNP (45 animals)50 animals (one-off)£3,500 one-off

Genetics sequencing is done once per animal — the result is permanent. Email ingestion and vault automation is built in-house at zero direct cost. Soil uses composite samples (20–25 cores bulked per test), not one test per hectare — the 4-year quadrant rotation keeps annual cost low while covering the whole farm over time.

Annual recurring spend

Four recurring programmes — genetics excluded as a one-off.

Practical recommendation: start with full testing in two quadrants for 1–2 months (pilot), tune sampling density and ingestion schemas, then scale farm-wide. This avoids over-sampling cost while still building a high-quality baseline for the vault.
