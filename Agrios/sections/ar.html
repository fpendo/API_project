EID tag recognition

The glasses read LoRa EID signals in range and pull each animal's record from the vault — no manual scanning needed.

GPS-anchored soil grid

Management cell boundaries are projected onto the field in real time — the operator walks to the crosshair and the bag is geo-logged on scan.

Live vault sync

Annotations in the HUD are pulled fresh from the Obsidian vault over the mesh — always current, whether that's a new test result or a fresh vet note.

QR sample logging

Scanning a bag QR code auto-creates a vault entry with GPS co-ordinates, cell ID, operator and timestamp — no paperwork, no transcription errors.

Priority alerts

Animals with active health flags, overdue vaccinations or breached geofences are surfaced as they enter the field of view — nothing to check, nothing to miss.

Hands-free voice control

Whisper STT on the glasses lets the operator dictate notes, confirm actions and ask questions — gloves on, in any weather.

Hardware concept: Any lightweight LoRa-paired AR headset (e.g. XReal, Vuzix, or custom open-frame) running a lightweight client that queries the Gupworthy Farm vault via the mesh network. The glasses receive EID uplinks from the same gateways already on the farm — no separate infrastructure needed.
