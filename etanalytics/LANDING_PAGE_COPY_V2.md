**Badge:**

**Headline:**

*Rationale: Two clear business outcomes. "Know" is active (not passive "see"). "Grow" speaks directly to their KPI. The line break creates visual hierarchy and makes it scannable.*

- "Your £5B in AUM deserves to know its owners."
- "Stop guessing who owns your ETFs."
- "The distribution intelligence your board has been asking for."

**Subheadline:**
```

identify who's buying, who's selling, and where to focus your distribution.
```
*Rationale: Opens with the pain point ("can't name your top 10 investors"), establishes empathy, then delivers the promise. Specific outcomes (buying, selling, distribution focus) make it tangible.*

**Primary CTA:**
```
See Who Owns Your ETFs →
```
*Rationale: Outcome-focused, specific action. Uses arrow to indicate forward motion. More compelling than generic "Get Started".*

**Secondary CTA:**
```
▶ Explore the Demo (No signup required)
```
*Rationale: Reduces friction by emphasizing "no signup required". Makes it feel risk-free.*

**Copywriting Principles Used:**
- **Problem-Agitation-Solution (PAS)** - Opens with problem ("can't name"), agitates it ("billions in assets"), offers solution
- **Specificity** - "Top 10 investors" is more vivid than "who owns your funds"
- **Outcome framing** - CTAs focus on what they GET, not what they DO

**Expected Impact:** +15-25% click-through on CTAs due to specificity and reduced friction on demo CTA.
